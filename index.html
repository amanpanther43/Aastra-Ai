Setup

Hardware Requirements

💻
Computer or Raspberry Pi
Any modern PC, Mac, or Linux machine. For portable deployment, Raspberry Pi 4 (4GB+ RAM) is recommended. An NVIDIA GPU is optional but greatly speeds up vision AI.
🎤
Microphone
A USB or 3.5mm microphone. Works best with a directional mic placed near the user. Microphone arrays are supported for whole-room coverage.
📷
Camera
Any USB webcam or IP camera stream. Minimum 720p recommended for facial recognition. Multiple cameras can be added for broader coverage.
📡
ESP32 Microcontrollers (Optional)
Required only for smart home automation. Flash with provided firmware and connect to the same WiFi network. Supports lights, relays, sensors, and more.
🔧
Python 3.11+ Environment
Install all dependencies via pip. Models download automatically on first run. Fully supports Windows, macOS, and Linux.

How It Works

Five Layers of Protection

1

Facial Recognition Gate

Every person entering camera view is checked against a known-faces database. Unknown individuals immediately trigger a configurable alert via push, email, or SMS.

2

Motion & Anomaly Detection

YOLOv8 continuously scans camera feeds for unusual objects and movements. The model learns what "normal" looks like in your home and flags any deviation.

3

Voice Authentication

Sensitive commands — like unlocking doors or disabling cameras — require voice biometric verification. Your voice is your password.

4

Encrypted Local Storage

All recordings, logs, and personal data are stored encrypted on local hardware. Nothing is sent to the cloud without your explicit permission.

5

Smart Alert Routing

Alerts are routed by severity: minor events log silently, moderate events trigger push notifications, and critical events send calls or SMS immediately.
